Artist Profile

Vera
Solano

Born in Buenos Aires, based between Berlin and New York. Solano's practice spans painting, installation, and digital sculpture — united by an uncompromising refusal to resolve tension. Her work has been described as "violence rendered tender" — an examination of systemic fracture through the lens of visceral, material beauty.

Recipient of the Frieze Foundation Prize (2024). Exhibited at MoMA PS1, Tate Modern, and Galerie Perrotin. Her process is irreversible: she builds to destroy, deconstructs to reveal.

MANIFESTO
Manifesto
Artist Statement
—
2026
Art must rupture the agreement between viewer and surface. It should leave you unsettled, rearranged — not in the comfortable way of a museum's white walls, but in the way of stepping off a moving train mid-sentence.
I

Refuse Resolution

Every artwork that resolves neatly into meaning is a lie. The most honest works remain open wounds — demanding engagement rather than admiration.

II

Embrace Failure

The accidental mark, the miscalculated gesture, the catastrophic overworking — these are not mistakes. They are the work revealing itself.

III

Destroy to See

Completion is the enemy of truth. I build structures only to interrogate what remains when they are taken apart, piece by methodical piece.

Behind the Work

THE
PROCESS

No work begins with certainty. Each piece originates in an unresolved question — a feeling that resists articulation until the materials begin to answer for themselves. The studio is not a controlled environment. It is a field of productive chaos where intuition and methodology collide.

Studio, Berlin 2026
01
Accumulation

Months of collecting — images, textures, fragments of thought. No formal sketching. Saturation before selection.

02
Rupture

First contact with surface. Uncontrolled, instinctual. The work begins in violence, then finds its own logic.

03
Interrogation

Weeks of looking, testing, refusing resolution. The work is finished when it stops asking for more.
